.................................................... 26 Page 1 Constitution of the United Empire of Arina Preamble Conscious of our responsibilities to the emperor and to the people of Arina, we the People of Arina establish this Constitution as the supreme law of the Empire. This Constitution shall guide Arina toward righteousness, order, and unity under Imperial ideals of honor, loyalty, and truth and affirms the Empire’s unity and continuity under the emperor’s sovereignty and guarantees justice, fundamental rights, and lawful governance for all citizens. Chapter I — The Emperor Article 1 — The emperor: The Emperor is the sovereign (Head of State) of the United Empire of Arina, representating the unity and continuity of the state. All state authority. Executive, Legislative, and Judicial, derives from the emperor and people. The emperor himself is inviolable. No Imperial institution may coerce or prosecute the emperor. He shall govern for the welfare, security, and prosperity of the Empire and its people. Article 2 — Sovereignty and Law: Sovereignty resides in the emperor alone, and is subject to this Constitution. The emperor may promulgate laws in His name and no other authority may enact laws or decrees that contradict the Constitution. All laws, edicts, and regulations require the emperor’s signature or seal to take effect. Page 2 Article 3 — Coronation and Imperial Oath: The Emperor is proclaimed at the Imperial Coronation according to the established hereditary succession or designation procedure. Upon accession, the Emperor takes the Coronation Oath before the Constitutional Court or a council of notables, pledging to uphold the Constitution and protect the Empire’s integrity and dignity. The text of this oath is prescribed by law and symbolizes the Emperor’s commitment to the people and the Constitution. Chapter II — Principles and Structure of Governance Article 4 — Principles of Governance: The Empire is governed by justice, order, loyalty, and lawful authority, adhering to the rule of law. Imperial governance aims to maintain peace, security, and stability for all citizens, ensuring equality before the law and protection of fundamental rights within the bounds of public order. All state institutions and officials serve the Empire and its people; abuse of public office is forbidden and is punished by law. Article 5 — Imperial Decrees and Legislation: The Emperor has the power to issue Imperial Decrees on any matter of state; such decrees become law once formally promulgated. Each decree must state its legal authority, scope, and effective date and be published in the official Imperial Gazette. The preparation of decrees is managed by the Imperial Council of Ministers (Article 29). Any decree affecting a specific department must be Page 3 countersigned by the responsible Minister to attest to its legality and execution. Decrees may amend or repeal existing laws, but they cannot alter this Constitution except through the formal amendment procedure. Challenges to the validity of a decree may be brought only before the Constitutional Court according to law. Article 6 — Imperial Parliament: The Empire’s legislative power is vested in the Imperial Parliament, a representative assembly established by law. Parliament enacts laws in the Emperor’s name subject to this Constitution. Members of Parliament serve four-month terms. Each member may serve only two consecutive terms; after two terms they must step down for at least one full term before being eligible again. This limitation ensures rotation and prevents any one person from holding power too long. Parliament’s internal organization and the method of selecting its members are prescribed by law. Every law passed by Parliament must be countersigned by the competent Minister, confirming its implementation. Article 7 — Nobility and Provincial Authority: The Empire comprises provinces governed by nobles or appointed Governors and Administrators under Imperial authority. Nobles and Governors oversee local administration, law enforcement, and public order in their territories, subject to Imperial law. Provincial and municipal authorities must faithfully execute Imperial laws and policies; they may enact local regulations Page 4 only insofar as they do not conflict with Imperial law or Imperial decrees. Nobles and local officials remain personally accountable to the Emperor; any abuse or neglect of their delegated authority is punished under Imperial law. Nobles owe military service and counsel to the Emperor and may be called to serve in the defense of the Empire. Article 8 — Strategic Headquarters and Armed Forces: The Strategic Headquarters is the central command for the Imperial Armed Forces, operating under the direct authority of the Emperor and the Five Grandmasters. The Armed Forces—including the Army, Navy, Air Service, and the Imperial Guard—are organized and maintained for the defense of the Empire and the enforcement of Imperial law. The Five Grandmasters of Arina form the High Military Council; they are senior commanders appointed by the Emperor to advise on defense, plan military strategy, and lead the Armed Forces. The Emperor is the supreme Commander-in-Chief of the Armed Forces. All military orders and actions require the Emperor’s authority and must align with the Empire’s defense, stability, and prosperity. Conscription and military service are duties of all able-bodied citizens as prescribed by law, with conditions defined by regulation. Chapter III — Citizenship, Nationality, and Oath Article 9 — Definition of Citizenship: A person is a citizen of the United Empire of Arina by birth, descent, naturalization, or Page 5 by Imperial decree as prescribed by law. Citizenship by birth is granted to anyone born to at least one Arina citizen parent. A child born in the Empire to stateless parents or to parents of unknown nationality acquires Arina citizenship at birth. Foreign nationals may acquire citizenship through naturalization under conditions set by law, such as residency, allegiance to the Emperor, and knowledge of Imperial duties. Dual citizenship is allowed only with special permission from the Emperor and may be restricted to protect Imperial interests. No citizen may be arbitrarily deprived of citizenship; it may be revoked only by due process of law for grave offenses like treason or espionage. Article 10 — Rights and Duties of Citizens: All citizens have the right to vote and hold public office as prescribed by law, provided they meet any qualifications established by statute. Citizens owe loyalty to the Empire and the Emperor. They have duties to defend the Empire, pay taxes, and serve in public positions if required by law. All citizens must obey the Constitution, Imperial laws, and lawful orders of public authorities. Within these bounds, citizens are free to voice concerns and participate in civic affairs; their loyalty cannot be coerced beyond the obligations stated here. Article 11 — Imperial Oath: All individuals appointed to high Imperial office (including ministers, judges, military officers, and governors) shall take the following oath before assuming their duties: Page 6 “I, [Name], solemnly pledge to serve the United Empire of Arina and its people. I shall uphold this Constitution and Imperial law, protect the Empire, and place its welfare above my own. I swear loyalty to the Emperor and obedience to lawful authority.” This Imperial Oath is legally binding. Anyone who willfully violates this oath in the exercise of official duties is subject to punishment under Imperial law. Chapter IV — Fundamental Rights and Freedoms Article 12 — Human Dignity: Human dignity is inviolable and must be respected and protected by all state authority. No law, decree, or state action shall diminish the inherent worth of the human person. The Empire exists to serve and protect human dignity. Article 13 — Equality Before the Law: All persons are equal before the law. Discrimination on the basis of birth, origin, language, beliefs, gender, status, or any other personal characteristic is prohibited unless explicitly justified by law for the protection of the Empire and public order. Everyone enjoys the same civil and political rights without unreasonable distinction. The state shall promote equal opportunities for all citizens. Article 14 — Right to Life and Personal Liberty: Every person has the right to life and bodily integrity. The taking of life is punishable by law except in cases expressly permitted by law Page 7 (such as lawful self-defense or in military action). Personal freedom is guaranteed. No one may be detained, imprisoned, or subjected to physical punishment or torture except in accordance with law and judicial procedure. The death penalty may be imposed only for the most serious crimes and only by final judgment of a competent court. Article 15 — Free Development of Personality: Every person has the right to the free development of their personality, provided they do not infringe on the rights of others or violate Arina’s constitutional order. Limitations on personal freedom must be provided by law and be necessary in a democratic society for public safety, health, or the rights of others. Article 16 — Freedom of Belief, Conscience, and Religion: Freedom of thought, belief, conscience, and religious practice is guaranteed to all. Each person may adhere to a faith of their choice or profess no faith, and may practice religion either alone or with others, in public or in private. No one may be compelled to participate in religious observance or to profess a belief against their will. State and religious institutions are separate, except that by law the Emperor may hold a ceremonial religious role as head of state. Religious practices may be limited by law if they threaten public order, safety, health, or the rights of others. Organizations that promote coercion, criminal conduct, or subversion of the state may be restricted or banned under law. Page 8 Article 17 — Freedom of Expression and Information: Everyone has the right to express and disseminate opinions, to publish and broadcast, and to receive information without interference. Freedom of the press and media is guaranteed by law. Censorship of the press is prohibited, though media may be regulated to protect youth, national security, or public order. Using media to incite violence, hatred, or unlawful acts is strictly prohibited. Article 18 — Freedom of Assembly and Association: All persons have the right to assemble peacefully and unarmed. No special permission is required for lawful assemblies, except as provided by law to preserve order. Everyone has the right to form associations, societies, and organizations. Associations that pursue illegal goals or act against the constitutional order may be prohibited by law. Article 19 — Privacy and Communication: The privacy of the home, correspondence, communication (including electronic communication), and personal data is guaranteed. Searches, seizures, wiretaps, and surveillance require judicial authorization and must be based on legal standards. Unauthorized disclosure of private information (doxxing) is strictly forbidden. The confidentiality of journalistic sources is protected by law, except when a compelling public interest requires disclosure by order of a court. Page 9 Article 20 — Marriage, Family, and Children: The institution of marriage and the family are under the special protection of the state. Parents have the natural right and duty to care for and educate their children, under the supervision of the state. Men and women have equal rights and duties as spouses. Divorce, guardianship, and adoption are regulated by law in the interest of the welfare of children and the integrity of the family. Article 21 — Education, Science, and Culture: Everyone has the right to education. Education shall be supervised by the Empire to ensure standards of quality, loyalty, and moral instruction. Parents have the right to choose the kind of education for their children within the law. Science, research, art, literature, and teaching shall be free, and cultural heritage shall be protected. Academic institutions enjoy autonomy under law but must pledge loyalty to Arina’s constitutional order. Article 22 — Property, Labor, and Economic Freedom: Property and inheritance rights are guaranteed. Expropriation of private property for public use is allowed only by law and with full, fair, and prompt compensation. The amount of compensation shall be justly determined by law, reflecting current market value. At the same time, property rights carry a social obligation: excessive accumulation or exploitation of property against the public good may be regulated by law. Every citizen has the right to work and to engage in lawful enterprise. The Empire shall ensure Page 10 conditions for fair labor, social welfare, and protection against exploitation. Article 23 — Petition and Legal Remedy: Everyone has the right to submit petitions, complaints, or requests to the Emperor and to Imperial authorities. Petitions may be made individually or jointly for the protection of one’s rights or in the common interest. Any person whose rights have been violated by public authority has the right to a judicial remedy. Courts shall provide effective relief without undue delay. Article 24 — Rule of Law and Due Process: The administration of justice is based on the rule of law. All government action must have a legal basis, and no one may be punished except according to law. Judges are independent and subject only to this Constitution and the law; they may not follow any orders beyond the law. No one may be punished without a final judgment by a competent court. Retroactive penal laws are prohibited. The accused are presumed innocent until proven guilty. Article 25 — Defense of the Constitutional Order: The existence of the constitutional order is guaranteed. Any actions seeking to abolish or undermine this Constitution or the Emperor’s sovereignty are prohibited. The Empire may restrict or suspend certain rights only as strictly necessary to prevent attempts to overthrow the constitutional order. All citizens have the right Page 11 and duty to resist anyone seeking to destroy the constitutional order of the Empire if no legal remedy exists. Article 26 — Eternity Clause: The following fundamental principles are inviolable and may not be amended or repealed under any circumstances: (a) Human dignity; (b) The inviolability of human and fundamental rights as laid out in this Constitution; (c) The unity and continuity of the United Empire of Arina; (d) The sovereignty of the Emperor as defined by this Constitution. No state authority may undermine these principles even temporarily, including during a state of emergency. Chapter V — Criminal Law Article 27 — Crimes Against the Empire: Crimes against the United Empire of Arina consist of acts that violate Imperial law or the security of the state, including but not limited to: (a) sabotage or destruction of Imperial property, infrastructure, or resources; (b) undermining the economy or national integrity through fraud, espionage, or corruption; (c) violent rebellion or insurrection against Imperial authority; (d) any act of terrorism or collusion with hostile powers against the Empire. Punishments for these crimes shall be prescribed by law and be proportionate to the offense. Article 28 — Treason: Treason against the Empire consists of deliberate acts intended to: (a) overthrow the Emperor or Imperial authority; (b) seize or usurp the Imperial Throne; (c) Page 12 assist or conspire with enemy powers to the detriment of the Empire; (d) sever the unity or continuity of the United Empire of Arina. Treason is the gravest crime and shall be punished with the harshest penalties allowed by law, including life imprisonment or death, upon final judgment by a competent court. Article 29 — High Crimes and Penalties: High crimes include: (a) forgery, counterfeiting, or embezzlement of Imperial currency or funds; (b) severe abuse of public office, including corruption and betrayal of official duty; (c) large-scale violations of public trust or subversion of legal processes. High crimes shall incur severe penalties short of those reserved for treason, as determined by law. All criminal punishments must conform to human dignity; torture and cruel or unusual punishments are strictly prohibited. Chapter VI — Government and Administration Article 30 — Imperial Council of Ministers: The Imperial Council of Ministers (the Cabinet) is the principal executive body assisting the Emperor in administering the Empire. It is chaired by the Imperial Chancellor. The Imperial Chancellor is appointed by the Emperor and must be a trusted Arina citizen of legal age and experience. On the Chancellor’s recommendation, the Emperor appoints the other ministers. Each Minister heads a government department (e.g., Finance, Defense, Foreign Affairs, Justice, Interior, Education, etc.) and is responsible for Page 13 implementing the Emperor’s policies and laws in that domain. Ministers are collectively responsible to the Emperor for executing the laws. The Emperor may dismiss any minister for loss of confidence, incompetence, or misconduct. Every Imperial Decree or law that regulates a specific sector must be countersigned by the competent Minister, confirming its implementation. Article 31 — Imperial Chancellor: The Imperial Chancellor leads the Council of Ministers and is the Emperor’s chief advisor. The Chancellor serves a term of four months, renewable only once. In other words, no one may serve as Chancellor for more than two consecutive terms; after serving two terms, the Chancellor must step down for at least one full term before being eligible again. This limitation ensures both stability and accountability at the highest level of government. Article 32 — Civil Service and Administration: The permanent civil service serves the Empire under the Constitution and the law. Civil servants are selected on the basis of merit and are expected to perform their duties impartially, loyal to the Constitution and the Emperor. The status, rights, and duties of civil servants are regulated by statute. Civil servants may not engage in partisan political activities that compromise their neutrality. Administrative procedures and decisions shall be governed by law. Citizens have the right to administrative appeal and to challenge bureaucratic decisions before the courts. Page 14 Article 33 — Provincial and Local Government: Provinces are administrative divisions of the Empire with internal self-government in domestic affairs, subject to the Constitution and Imperial authority. They may manage local administration, public services, finances, development, education, culture, and policing under Imperial standards. All provincial laws must conform to Imperial law. Provinces may not conduct foreign relations, maintain armed forces, issue currency, or exercise powers reserved to the Emperor or the Imperial government. The Emperor may intervene to restore lawful governance, and disputes shall be settled by the Constitutional Court with final authority. Chapter VII — Defense and Public Order Article 34 — National Defense: The defense of the Empire is the supreme duty of every citizen. Only the Emperor may declare war, conclude peace, or enter into treaties of alliance or guarantee. The Emperor’s decision to mobilize the Armed Forces is based on national interest and strategic necessity. The scope and duration of any mobilization are defined by Imperial decree or law. The Armed Forces shall defend the homeland, maintain security, and assist in disaster relief. Military engagements abroad require a statement of purpose from the Page 15 Emperor and may be subject to approval by the Parliament if one exists. Article 35 — Law Enforcement and Internal Security: The Imperial Guard and Imperial Police are responsible for maintaining public order and enforcing the law. These forces operate under the Emperor’s command and are subject to the Constitution. Provincial and municipal police forces exist under local authorities but must also abide by Imperial standards and coordinate with national security interests. Citizens are obligated to assist the police and other law enforcement officials, within legal limits, in preventing and combating crime. Any abuse of force or authority by law enforcement officials may be challenged in court and punished by law. Prisons and detention facilities are under the oversight of the judiciary to ensure that human rights are protected. Chapter VIII — Economy and Public Finance Article 36 — Economic and Fiscal Authority: The Emperor directs and supervises economic and fiscal policy, aiming to ensure prosperity, social welfare, and the public interest. Trade, industry, agriculture, and entrepreneurship are encouraged under Imperial protection and regulation. The state may promote social and economic objectives and support public services or enterprises as needed by law. The Imperial Treasury manages the national finances. The fiscal year is set by Imperial decree. No taxes may be imposed or collected without an Imperial Page 16 decree or law. Taxes must be fair, publicly announced, and proportionate to the taxpayer’s ability to pay. Article 37 — Budget and Public Expenditure: The Imperial government shall draft an annual budget detailing projected revenues and planned expenditures. The budget must be presented to the Constitutional Court and, if one exists, to any consultative assembly or council for review before implementation. Public expenditures shall be limited to those authorized by the budget. No spending may exceed the budgeted amounts without an emergency or supplementary decree approved by the Emperor. Public debt shall be regulated by law. Borrowing must be fiscally responsible, and no excessive debt shall endanger future generations. Article 38 — Property and Expropriation: Citizens and corporations have the right to own property. Property rights entail obligations to the public good. Expropriation of private property for public use is permitted only by law and with full, fair, and prompt compensation. The amount of compensation shall be justly determined by law, reflecting current market value. Chapter IX — Foreign Relations and International Law Article 39 — Diplomatic Relations and Treaties: Only the Emperor has the authority to establish, maintain, or end diplomatic relations with other states. Ambassadors and envoys Page 17 are accredited by the Emperor. Treaties and international agreements are negotiated and signed in the Emperor’s name. Any treaty that encroaches on Imperial sovereignty or fundamental rights must receive formal approval by the Emperor and, if constituted, by the consultative council or parliament. International law and treaties ratified by the Empire are part of the supreme law of the Empire, taking precedence over Imperial laws as provided by the terms of ratification. Article 40 — Status of Foreigners: Foreign nationals within the Empire enjoy the protection of Imperial law and may do business under the same rules as citizens, subject to conditions set by law. Foreign diplomatic and consular personnel have immunity to the extent prescribed by international law and custom. The embassies of foreign powers are inviolable in accordance with diplomatic conventions. Foreign mercenaries, spies, or agents who engage in unauthorized military or intelligence operations within the Empire shall be subject to severe penalties under Imperial law. Chapter X — Communications and Digital Media Article 41 — Freedom and Regulation of Communications: The Empire guarantees freedom of speech, press, and digital communication, allowing free exchange of ideas and information. The use of media and communication tools for harassment, threats, defamation, or propagation of violence, hatred, or insurgency is prohibited and punishable by law. The Page 18 state may establish independent regulatory bodies to oversee radio, television, and internet services to ensure accuracy, protect minors, and secure national information infrastructure. Article 42 — Privacy of Electronic Data: Citizens have the right to privacy in their personal communications and digital data. Electronic surveillance by the state may only occur with judicial authorization and for specific purposes such as national security or criminal investigation. Doxxing, hacking, or any unauthorized collection or dissemination of private personal data is strictly forbidden and shall be punished by law. Internet neutrality and open access shall be protected by law, subject only to restrictions necessary for public security and prevention of criminal use. Chapter XI — Succession and Continuity Article 43 — Line of Succession: The Emperor’s throne passes according to established hereditary succession rules. Primogeniture applies unless otherwise specified by Imperial law. The Emperor may designate a successor (Heir Apparent) by formal decree during His lifetime. If the Emperor dies or abdicates without a designated heir, the next in the hereditary line shall succeed. The Constitutional Court shall confirm the legitimacy of succession in any disputed case. Article 44 — Regency: If the Emperor is unable to discharge the duties of the throne due to minority, illness, or absence, a Page 19 Regency Council shall govern temporarily in the name of the Emperor. The Regency Council consists of senior nobles, the Imperial Chancellor, and judges as designated by law, ensuring no single group holds a majority. Regents may exercise only those powers necessary to maintain governance and may not make final decisions on succession or constitutional amendments. The Regency ends immediately when the Emperor or the designated heir is able to assume power. Article 45 — Oath of the Emperor: Every Emperor, upon accession, shall take the Imperial Coronation Oath (Article 3) affirming loyalty to the Constitution and the well-being of the Empire. The authority of an Emperor who abdicates, is deposed, or is officially declared dead lapses immediately, and succession or regency proceeds according to this Constitution. Article 46 — Illegitimacy of Usurpation: Any claim to the Throne not in accordance with the laws of succession is considered an act of treason. Such claimants and their supporters shall be subject to the penalties for treason under this Constitution. Chapter XII — Oversight, Checks, and State of Emergency Article 47 — Constitutional Court: A Constitutional Court is hereby established as the supreme guardian of this Constitution. It exercises constitutional jurisdiction in the name of the Emperor. The Court consists of an odd number of judges (not Page 20 fewer than seven), appointed by the Emperor for non-renewable terms fixed by law. Judges must be distinguished legal experts committed to the Constitution. The Constitutional Court is independent and bound only by the Constitution and the law; it may not be influenced by any other authority. Article 48 — Jurisdiction of the Constitutional Court: The Constitutional Court reviews the constitutionality of laws, Imperial decrees, regulations, and emergency measures. Any citizen who has exhausted ordinary legal remedies may petition the Court for protection of fundamental rights. The Court resolves disputes between Imperial institutions and between the Empire and its provinces. Its rulings are final and binding; any act found unconstitutional has no legal effect. The Emperor may issue a corrective decree to enforce the Court’s decision, but only to the extent necessary to give effect to the judgment. Article 49 — Impeachment: Ministers or other high officials who violate this Constitution or their Imperial Oath may be impeached under procedures defined by law. If the Emperor commits an offense against the constitutional order (e.g., treason), the Constitutional Court together with the Council of Ministers and any consultative council may confirm the breach by an eighty percent (80%) supermajority vote. In such a case, a Declaration of Imperial Default may be proclaimed to remove the Emperor from power and restore lawful governance. An Page 21 interim ruler or council shall be appointed to oversee the selection of a new Emperor according to the rules of succession. Article 50 — State of Emergency: The Emperor may declare a State of Emergency in cases of war, armed insurrection, rebellion, foreign aggression, or natural catastrophe that threatens the existence of the Empire or public order. Emergency measures may include temporary suspension of certain rights (only as strictly necessary), immediate decrees, and mobilization of the Armed Forces or police to restore order. A State of Emergency expires after thirty (30) days unless extended by Imperial decree, which must be justified by the Emperor and may be reviewed by the Constitutional Court. Even in an emergency, human dignity and the core principles of the Constitution remain inviolable. The Emperor must report the status of any emergency to the Constitutional Court or an appointed oversight body at regular intervals. Article 51 — Abolition of the Constitutional Order: Any attempt to abolish or radically alter the constitutional order without following the amendment procedures is unlawful and shall be considered treason. No person or authority may suspend rights to freedom and the rule of law in order to undermine the Constitution, except as provided by law and necessity under a duly declared state of emergency. Article 52 — Accountability: No official, including the Emperor, is above the law. Violations of this Constitution or Imperial law Page 22 by any official can be tried by the courts or through special procedures as determined by law. Judges and public prosecutors have a duty to bring serious allegations of constitutional or legal violations by officials, including the Emperor, to the attention of the Constitutional Court or the appropriate authorities. Chapter XIII — Special Administrative Regions Article 53 — Status: Any Administrative Region of the Empire (a “SAR”) derives its sovereignty from the Emperor, who delegates certain self-governing powers under Imperial law. Each SAR has a Basic Law as its organic law, operating under this Constitution. Imperial laws and this Constitution take precedence over any local provisions in case of conflict. Article 54 — Rights and Duties in the SARs: All fundamental rights guaranteed in this Constitution apply to residents of the SARs, including equality before the law and protection from harm. Residents of a SAR have freedom of speech and may criticize local authorities, but treason against the Empire or the SAR is prohibited. Freedom of religion is guaranteed; religious practices that threaten public order, involve coercive elements, or violate others’ rights may be restricted by regional law in conformity with Imperial directives. Private property is protected by law in SARs; no person may be detained without charge or due process. Non-discrimination on the basis of ethnicity, religion, or origin is guaranteed in the SARs. Page 23 Article 55 — The Viceroy of a SAR: The Viceroy is the head of a SAR and exercises executive authority in the region under the Emperor’s delegation. The Viceroy must be an Arina citizen and is elected by the people of the SAR in free and fair elections, subject to confirmation by the Emperor or the Constitutional Court to ensure constitutional compliance. The Viceroy is a member of the SAR Council (Article 56) and presides over it. The Viceroy faithfully executes Imperial and regional laws, promotes public welfare, and ensures security within the SAR. The Viceroy may be removed from office for treason, violation of the Constitution, incapacity, or by recall referendum as provided by regional law. Article 56 — The Council of a SAR: The Legislative Council of a SAR consists of nine members, including the Viceroy. Members are elected by the people of the SAR for fixed terms, with qualifications and election procedures defined by regional law under Imperial oversight. The Council drafts and enacts local regulations, budgets, and development plans for the SAR, subject to Imperial law. It supervises the Viceroy’s administration and approves regional appointments. A majority of the Council (five of nine) is needed to pass ordinary measures; amending the Basic Law requires a three-quarters (¾) supermajority of the Council. The Viceroy may veto Council decisions with written justification; the Council can override a veto with a two-thirds (⅔) majority. Page 24 Article 57 — Emergency Powers in SARs: If war, disaster, rebellion, or breakdown of order occurs in a SAR, the Viceroy or the Emperor may declare a local State of Emergency. Such a declaration must specify the reasons and measures to be taken. During a local emergency, the Viceroy may issue emergency decrees restricting certain rights or mobilizing local security forces, subject to review by Imperial authorities. Measures must be necessary, proportionate, and time-limited. A SAR state of emergency lasts no more than thirty (30) days unless extended by the Emperor or Imperial Strategic Headquarters. All emergency measures expire when the emergency ends. Article 58 — Treason in the SARs: In addition to Imperial treason laws (Chapter V, Article 28), treason in a SAR is defined as deliberate acts aimed at overthrowing or harming the Empire or the SAR’s constitutional order through force or collusion with enemies. No treason accusation shall proceed without sufficient evidence and due legal process. Punishment for treasonous acts against a SAR or the Empire shall be severe, up to life imprisonment or death as prescribed by Imperial law. Article 59 — Amendment Procedure for SAR Basic Laws: Amendments to a SAR’s Basic Law may be proposed by the Viceroy or any Council member. Amendments require the approval of at least three-quarters (¾) of the SAR Council and the assent of the Emperor. Ratified amendments take effect immediately or as otherwise specified. Page 25 Article 60 — Supremacy Clause for SARs: A SAR’s Basic Law and this Imperial Constitution supersede all prior regional laws. All provisions of the Basic Law shall be interpreted in harmony with Imperial law and this Constitution. In case of conflict, the Constitution of the United Empire of Arina prevails. Final Articles Article 61 — Constitution as Supreme Law: This Constitution, including the Preamble and all integrated Fundamental Laws and Basic Laws, is the supreme law of the United Empire of Arina. It binds the Emperor, all authorities, and all citizens. Any law, decree, treaty, or custom inconsistent with this Constitution is null and void. Article 62 — Oath of Allegiance: All officeholders and public servants shall take an oath or affirmation to uphold this Constitution and swear loyalty to the Emperor and the Empire.
